Preamble

This article is part of the lecture notes series authored for Sicss Logics Seminar 2016. This article covers the introduction part (Part 1) of the seminar’s agenda.

Abstract

The first article of the lecture notes series cover the significance of formal logic systems, a general and informal introduction to formal systems, introduction to the mathematical devices we are going to use to study the formal systems. More specifically, we are going to introduce the notations of sets, relations, functions, and devote significant space for the introduction of inductive definitions and induction principles.

Preface

The topic of this seminar, and thus this lecture notes series is the mathematical device of first order logic. Through this seminar, participants are going to be introduced with it, study several critical properties of it, and gain skills in formal reasoning in mathematics in general. This seminar, also this lecture notes series, assumes no preliminary background in mathematics other than the familiarities of the concepts of set and function, although even these concepts will be reintroduced. The participants are, however, expected to be passionate about this topic, and actively participate in seminars and its preparations. This seminar introduces a quite non-trivial intellectual device which is highly possible totally new to the participants, thus plenty of mental struggles are expected. But nevertheless, first order logic is one of the most widely employed devices in mathematics and it is almost as strong to encode all reasonings humankind could perform, mathematical or not. So if you survive the seminar, you will gain a lot.
